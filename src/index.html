About Viet Nam

Located in Southeast Asia, Vietnam is a small and beautiful country with victorious history, profound patriotism and wonderful landscapes. Exploring the beauty of the country with its people and culture should be done once in one’s lifetime.

  • Location: South East Asia
  • Population: About 100 million
  • Language: Vietnamese
  • Religion: Confucianism; Taoism; Buddhism, Roman Catholicism; Cao Daism

Having a tropical climate, Vietnam is well known for from magnificent scenery and colorful hill tribes to wide terraced fields in Red River Delta and Mekong River Delta, to majestic mountains, and white sandy beaches. Vietnam, nowadays, is one of should-not-miss destinations in Asia.

Why Vietnam is famous for tourist

  1. Architecture:

    Location at the crossroads of multiple civilizations has left its mark on the land.

    1.1. Temple Roof
  2. History:

    Vietnam's culture dates back to 2,000 BC. Like many other countries in the region, its history is complicated, starting from the Dong Son culture that spanned thousands of years, and moving through many different leaders, lords, and dynasties

    1.2. Dong Son Temple
  3. Food

    Vietnamese food is a culinary adventure waiting to happen. Whether you’re sampling sizzling street food in Hanoi, indulging in fresh seafood in Phu Quoc, or savouring a fragrant bowl of pho, your taste buds are in for a treat.

    1.3. Bun Cha

    Foodies around the world seek out authentic Vietnamese cuisine, because it's simply the best. It's always fresh, tasty and healthy. Whether you are eating at a food stall or in a restaurant, the food is heavenly.

    1.4. Bun Rieu

    Here are some of the yummy Vietnamese dishes that you should definitely try out apart from the Vietnamese Pho – Banh Mi, Bun Cha, Goi Cuon (Vietnamese clear spring rolls), Banh Bao, Bánh Tráng Nướng (Vietnamese Pizza), among others.

    1.5. Pho
  4. The Natural Beauty

    Vietnam is endowed with 3,000 kilometers of pristine tropical coastline, resulting in some of the best beaches in the world. Luckily most of its beaches aren't crowded with tourists like Thailand or Philippines, but we recommend going now before everyone finds out.

    1.6. Nha Trang

    The mountains here are otherworldly. Stretching from the east to the west coast and rising out of the ocean, these karst mountains need to be seen in person to be fully appreciated. Many hikers can spend their whole trip exploring these peaks via small roads and winding trails.

    1.7. Fansipan

    Put simply Vietnam's natural beauty is unmatched. It contains a handful of World Heritage Sites: Halong Bay, Phon Nha Ke Bang Caves, Hoi An Ancient Street and Cham Islands.

    1.8. Hoi An
  5. One of the best coffe in the world:

    The majority of coffee drunk in Vietnam is Robusta, a bean known for its high caffeine content so don’t be surprised if you get the jitters after a few cups!

    1.9. Vietnamese coffee

    Robusta can be bitter, so is often served with sweetened condensed milk to lighten the flavor
